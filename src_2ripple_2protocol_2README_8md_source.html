6 ## Serialized Objects
7 
8 In ripple objects transmitted over the network must be
9 serialized into a canonical format. The prefix "ST" refers
10 to classes that deal with the serialized format of ripple
11 objects.
12 
13 The term "Tx" or "tx" is an abbreviation for "Transaction",
14 a commonly occurring object type.
15 
16 ### Optional Fields
17 
18 Our serialized fields have some "type magic" to make
19 optional fields easier to read:
20 
21 - The operation `x[sfFoo]` means "return the value of 'Foo'
22  if it exists, or the default value if it doesn't."
23 - The operation `x[~sfFoo]` means "return the value of 'Foo'
24  if it exists, or nothing if it doesn't." This usage of the
25  tilde/bitwise NOT operator is not standard outside of the
26  `rippled` codebase.
27  - As a consequence of this, `x[~sfFoo] = y[~sfFoo]`
28  assigns the value of Foo from y to x, including omitting
29  Foo from x if it doesn't exist in y.
30 
31 Typically, for things that are guaranteed to exist, you use
32 `x[sfFoo]` and avoid having to deal with a container that may
33 or may not hold a value. For things not guaranteed to exist,
34 you use `x[~sfFoo]` because you want such a container. It
35 avoids having to look something up twice, once just to see if
36 it exists and a second time to get/set its value.
37 ([Real example](https://github.com/ripple/rippled/blob/35f4698aed5dce02f771b34cfbb690495cb5efcc/src/ripple/app/tx/impl/PayChan.cpp#L229-L236))
38 
39 The source of this "type magic" is in
40 [SField.h](./SField.h#L296-L302).
